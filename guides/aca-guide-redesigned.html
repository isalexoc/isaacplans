Understand Your Subsidy Options

Health insurance subsidies (also known as Advanced Premium Tax Credits) are one of the easiest ways to lower your monthly premiums. These subsidies are available to individuals and families who meet certain income requirements when purchasing coverage through the ACA Marketplace.

  • What are subsidies?
    They're financial assistance from the government that helps lower the cost of health insurance premiums.
  • How do you qualify?
    Subsidies are based on your income and household size compared to the federal poverty level. Many people are surprised to learn they qualify for subsidies, even if they think their income is too high.
  • How do you calculate your subsidy?
    Tools on the Marketplace website or assistance from an experienced agent can help you determine how much you're eligible for.

Choose The Right Plan For Your Needs

One size doesn't fit all when it comes to health insurance. Choosing the wrong plan can mean paying more than you need for benefits you won't use‚Äîor worse, ending up with unexpected expenses.

Evaluate the ACA Metal Tiers:

  • Bronze Plans: Lower premiums but higher out-of-pocket costs (great for healthy individuals who rarely need care).
  • Silver Plans: Moderate premiums and cost-sharing. Subsidies are often tied to this level.
  • Gold/Platinum Plans: Higher premiums but lower out-of-pocket costs (ideal for those with frequent medical needs).

Match Your Plan to Your Healthcare Needs:

Consider how often you visit the doctor, your prescription costs, and your anticipated medical needs for the year.

Maximize Preventative Care Benefits

The ACA requires health insurance plans to cover many preventive services at no cost to you. Using these services is not only good for your health but can also save you money by catching potential issues early.

  • Examples of Preventive Services: Annual check-ups, screenings (like mammograms or colonoscopies), immunizations, and more.
  • Why It Saves Money: Preventing health issues or detecting them early can help avoid expensive treatments down the road.

Use In-Network Providers

Insurance companies negotiate lower rates with doctors, hospitals, and other healthcare providers who are in-network. Using out-of-network providers can result in significantly higher costs.

  • What Does In-Network Mean? These are providers who have a contract with your insurance company.
  • Why It Matters: Out-of-network services can result in surprise bills that aren't fully covered by your insurance.
  • Pro Tip: Always check whether your doctor, hospital, or specialist is in-network before scheduling appointments.

Review and Update Your Plan Annually - It's Not Too Late

Your healthcare needs and financial situation change over time‚Äîand so do health insurance plans. That's why it's crucial to review your plan every year to ensure it still fits your needs.

  • Open Enrollment Period: This is the annual window when you can switch plans (typically November 1‚ÄìJanuary 15).
  • Special Enrollment Period (SEP): You may qualify to change your plan outside of Open Enrollment if you've experienced a qualifying life event like:
    • Loss of previous coverage.
    • Marriage or divorce.
    • Having a baby or adopting a child.
    • Moving to a new area.
  • Why Review Your Plan? Plans, premiums, networks, and subsidies can all change. Reviewing your plan ensures you're not paying more than you need or missing out on better options.

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoiding Scams and Misleading Practices

Health Insurance is an important decision, and unfortunately, there are dishonest individuals and companies out there who may try to take advantage of that.

Be cautious of:

  • High-pressure tactics or promises that sound too good to be true (guaranteed $0 health insurance, gift cards, restaurant cards, visa cards, etc)
  • Emphasis on freebies or giveaways rather than providing valuable insurance guidance: These tactics are often designed solely to collect your personal information without offering meaningful assistance.
  • Companies or individuals asking for your personal information without clearly explaining how it will be used.
  • Offers that focus solely on signing you up quickly rather than helping you understand your options.

üö® Agencies Offering Enrollment Without Speaking To An Agent

Be cautious of agencies or individuals who offer to enroll you in a health plan without a personal consultation. This approach is often designed to quickly "flip" you into a plan for the sole purpose of earning a commission, rather than ensuring the plan is the right fit for your needs.

Avoid sharing your personal information with these companies and be wary of providing consent for them to make changes to your plan without thoroughly explaining your options. Your health coverage is too important to be handled by someone who doesn't take the time to understand your unique needs.
